Vincent van Gogh

Among the most famous figures in Western art, Van Gogh (1853-1890) created over 2,100 artworks marked by bold colours and dramatic brushwork.

The Arles Years

Van Gogh’s two years in Arles (1888–89) were among his most productive. He was inspired by the countryside and light of southern France.

One of his most famous works from this period is Bedroom in Arles.

On view: Vincent van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam

The Yellow House

During his time in Arles, Van Gogh lived in a rented home known as the Yellow House.

On view: Vincent van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam

The Cafe Terrace at Night

Van Gogh painted The Cafe Terrace at Night at Place du Forum in Arles.

On view: Kröller-Müller Museum, Otterlo

The Starry Night

After Arles, Van Gogh moved to Saint-Remy, where he created the iconic Starry Night.

On view: The Museum of Modern Art, New York

Van Gogh struggled with mental illness and poverty. He sold only one painting during his lifetime.
